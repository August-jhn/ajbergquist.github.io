Introduction

A knot is an embeding of the circle \(\mathbb{S}^1\hookrightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\). While each of these embedings are homeomorphic to each other, they can take on a variety of different shapes. For example, the trivial knot, which could be parametrized as \((cos\theta, \sin\theta), \theta \in [0,2\pi]\). (note that the 3d model of a knot is thickened so as to make it look nice. In reality, knots don't have any girth, and are a 1D object.)
